Turbo, Gas-Only, 5-Seat SUVs Under $50,000 — Alternatives to the Mazda CX-50

US market only, model years 2023–2026. One master table, sortable and filterable, rather than separate tables per segment.

  • "Gas-only" = turbocharged, explicitly non-hybrid/non-mild-hybrid/non-PHEV. Several models also sell a separate hybrid line (mirroring the CX-50's own gas Turbo vs. CX-50 Hybrid split). Volvo XC40/XC60, BMW X3, Mercedes GLC, and Mercedes GLB are excluded because their turbo engine comes with a standard 48V mild-hybrid system — not for lacking a turbo.
  • Qualifying threshold: at least one gas-only turbo trim priced under $50k somewhere in the lineup. No supercharged SUV meets these criteria in this market/window — every entry below is turbocharged.
  • Used Market Range: either KBB's "Fair Purchase Price" (national-average used-dealer transaction estimate) or a small sample of individual cars.com listings where KBB data wasn't available — the latter is a narrow local snapshot, not a market average, so treat those figures as rough indicators only.
  • Horsepower/Torque: dual figures (regular 87 octane vs. premium 93 octane) are shown only where the manufacturer officially publishes both — a documented Mazda SkyActiv-Turbo trait (CX-50, CX-30, CX-5), not found elsewhere in this list. Other engines that merely recommend premium without a separate reduced-fuel rating show a single official figure.
  • HP/Weight (lb per hp): curb weight ÷ horsepower, lower = more power-dense; sourced trim-by-trim (mostly via Car and Driver/MotorTrend/cars.com specs) and cited inline. A few rows are genuinely unresolved after extensive research and are marked "not found this session" rather than guessed — notably Kia Seltos SX AWD, Mazda CX-5 Turbo, and Cadillac XT4. A few others use a closest-available substitute because the row's exact trim/engine/drivetrain combination doesn't actually exist in that model year (flagged inline as "trim substitute" or "trim mismatch").
  • Safety Ratings: IIHS award / NHTSA star citations sourced via Wikipedia (IIHS.org and NHTSA.gov aren't reachable from this research environment). Wikipedia's safety sections are often several redesigns out of date, so every citation is labeled with the model year/generation it actually refers to, and flagged when that generation does not match the current MY2023–2026 vehicle in the row.
  • Warranty is shown at the brand level (basic/bumper-to-bumper · powertrain), since terms are set by the manufacturer rather than varying by trim.
